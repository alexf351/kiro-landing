How to choose an AI learning app

A strong AI learning app should teach durable mental models and current tools. It should help users practice prompting, compare outputs, notice hallucinations, and understand when a workflow is reliable enough to use.

For many learners, the deciding factor is consistency. Iro is built around short mobile sessions so users can keep practicing even when they do not have time for a long course.

What to look for

  • Short lessons that fit daily routines.
  • Hands-on exercises instead of passive video only.
  • Coverage of major tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity.
  • Practice with prompt engineering, agents, automation, and AI foundations.
  • Clear progress signals such as XP, streaks, ranks, or assessments.
  • Transparent pricing and easy cancellation through the platform store.

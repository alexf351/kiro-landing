How to read this list

Every AI tool you read about claims to be the best at everything. Most aren't. Each tool has a shape it's actually good at — and a place where it's a waste of attention.

This list is for the free tiers as of 2026. Paid plans often unlock significantly more, but the free tiers are enough to do real work if you know which one to reach for. The bigger skill is knowing when to use which.

Writing & general work

ChatGPT (free tier). Best default for drafting, summarising, brainstorming, and most writing tasks. Strong at structure. Iro AI has a dedicated ChatGPT path.

Claude (free tier). The one to reach for on long documents — pasted PDFs, long memos, multi-page edits. More careful and conservative than ChatGPT. Iro AI's Claude path drills its strengths.

Gemini (free tier). Strong when your work is already inside Google — Docs, Gmail, Sheets. Less useful as a standalone chat. Gemini path.

When to use which: ChatGPT for the first draft, Claude for the cleanup pass on anything long, Gemini if the document already lives in Google.

Research & sources

Perplexity (free tier). The default for anything that needs citations. Grounded in live web search, surfaces sources you can click. Catches most hallucinations by construction. Iro AI's Perplexity path teaches the workflow.

ChatGPT with browsing (free tier). Useful for ad-hoc research but the citation discipline is weaker than Perplexity's. Verify before quoting.

Claude with files (free tier). If your research is reading dense documents — earnings calls, legal filings, academic papers — Claude is the right tool. Paste the document, ask focused questions.

Coding

ChatGPT (free tier). Quick syntax questions, small functions, debugging help. Trust the structure, verify the details.

Claude (free tier). Best free option for longer code review and multi-file changes. Tends to catch edge cases other models miss.

Cursor (free tier). AI-first editor. Worth installing even if you only use the free tier — the in-editor experience is much faster than copying code in and out of a chat.

GitHub Copilot (free for students/OSS). Inline completion. Good for keeping flow when you already know what you want.

Images & video

Free image generation. Bing Image Creator (DALL·E 3 under the hood), Microsoft Designer, and the free tier of Flux on various hosts cover most needs. Iro AI's image generation course teaches transferable prompting skills.

Free video generation. Free tiers are constrained but useful: Runway's free clips, Luma's daily free generations, Pika's free credits. Skills transfer — see Iro AI's video generation course.

Caveat: the free tiers move fast. Treat any specific tool name in this list as a placeholder for whichever free tool currently fits the slot.

FAQ

Which single AI tool should I learn first if I can only pick one?

ChatGPT. Largest free tier, most general, easiest to find help. Once you're fluent, branch out to Claude and Perplexity.

Are the free tiers good enough for real work?

For most everyday tasks, yes. Pro tiers unlock faster models and longer contexts, which matter more for long documents and complex coding.

How do I stop my workflow from breaking when models update?

Build your skills around patterns, not magic prompts. Patterns survive updates.

What's missing from this list?

Plenty — Notion AI, Anthropic's API, OpenAI's playground, specialised tools like ElevenLabs, Suno, and so on. This list is the daily stack, not the encyclopedia.

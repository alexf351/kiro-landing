What they have in common

Coursiv and Learnova compete for the same person: someone who has heard they should "learn AI," wants a structured, beginner-friendly way to do it on their phone, and found both apps through ads. The overlap is real: both teach tools like ChatGPT through short, guided lessons; both are mobile-first; both use quizzes to check understanding; and both monetize through subscriptions.

Because of that overlap, the choice between them comes down to format details and pricing mechanics rather than radically different products. Here is the honest breakdown.

Where Coursiv wins

Coursiv's strength is momentum. The 28-day challenge framing gives beginners a clear container: open the app, do today's lesson, tick it off, repeat. Reviewers generally praise the clean design and low-pressure pace. If you're starting from zero and respond well to a fixed program with an end date, Coursiv's structure does that job.

Where Learnova wins

Breadth and checkpoints are where Learnova pulls ahead. Instead of one challenge, it breaks AI tools into a library of step-by-step courses with quizzes along the way and a hands-on playground to try things. It also offers a free basic tier, which makes it easier to evaluate before paying. If you want to browse a catalog and pick your own route through it, Learnova fits that shape better.

Pricing, honestly

This is where most of the frustration with both apps shows up in reviews, so read this part carefully.

Coursiv typically leads with a discounted introductory offer that then renews automatically as a recurring multi-week subscription. The intro price is not the ongoing price — track the renewal date and amount before you commit.

Learnova uses a more conventional tiered model: a free basic plan with limited access, plus premium subscriptions for the full catalog. Fairer on its face, but the same rule applies: check exactly what the free tier includes and what premium renews at.

Neither model is a scam; both are common in consumer apps. The pattern to avoid is subscribing on an intro price without knowing the renewal terms.

FAQ

Is Coursiv or Learnova better for learning AI?

They are closer than their ads suggest: both teach AI tools through short, guided, course-style lessons. Coursiv leans on a 28-day challenge format; Learnova is a tiered course library with quizzes and a practice playground. If you want structure and don't mind a subscription, either works. If you learn better by doing than by completing lessons, a practice-first app like Iro AI fits better.

Which is cheaper, Coursiv or Learnova?

It depends on renewal terms, which is where people get caught. Coursiv typically leads with a discounted introductory offer that auto-renews as a recurring multi-week subscription. Learnova uses a free basic tier plus premium subscriptions. Always check what the plan renews at before subscribing — to either, or to anything.

Do Coursiv or Learnova have free versions?

Learnova offers a free basic tier with limited access. Coursiv usually leads with a paid introductory offer rather than a free tier. Iro AI is free to start, and the free AI IQ test requires no signup at all.

What should I check before subscribing to any AI learning app?

## 5. HARO / Featured / Qwoted answer templates

**"How should beginners learn AI?"**
> The mistake most beginners make is consuming instead of practicing. Watching
> tutorials feels productive, but AI skills are motor skills: you learn
> prompting by writing prompts and getting feedback, the same way you learn a
> language by speaking it. Ten minutes of writing real prompts — and seeing
> why they fail — beats an hour of video. [Alex Furukawa, founder of Iro AI,
> a gamified app for practicing AI skills]

**"Is prompt engineering still a skill worth learning?"**
> The syntax tricks are dying, but the judgment isn't. Models need less magic
> phrasing every year — what still separates strong users is knowing what
> context to give, what to constrain, and when not to trust the output. We
> test thousands of people on this: the average professional scores about
> 5/10 on basic AI literacy. The gap isn't prompts, it's judgment. [Alex
> Furukawa, founder of Iro AI]
